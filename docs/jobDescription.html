Reason for this page

I made this page to inform potential employers on my previous job experiences. I myself have a hard time on the spot remembering everything that I have done for a company and thought writing it down would be helpful. So, I wrote it down where everyone can see it and read for themselves. I like to think of this page like a blog of all the jobs I have worked and how I have felt within the job. I want to look back at this and remember where I started and where I will end. It is my own like documentation of my own job history. I put a link on the photos for you to check out links to the places I have worked.

PARKER HANNIFIN CORPORATION | 07/2021 to 06/2023

At Parker Hannifin I worked in assembly at first. While working in assembly I followed rules, looked at materials/blueprints when needed, and positioned/algined components and parts. I enjoy my time there and the people are great. Everyone is friendly and helps each other when needed. Assembly taught me that hard work pays off and even if the job is not the greatest the people make it better.

As an HR Intern I would do a lot. I would Organize files from employee personal files to terminated files. I would organize files within the shared community folder on the cloud to make sure everything was up to date. I would edit the kiosk to inform employee's about new and upcoming events or make sign up sheets for particular events. I also made a prize for the workers when they had their own chili cook off.

Parker Hannifin is a huge corporation with buildings all over. The branch I worked for there was only four in the united states. This one in my home town was small and had two different locations. With the size of the company everyone knew everyone, it felt like one big family. Like any family we had our own issues and we would find people or go to the people that knew hoe to fix the issue. It is nice to work for a company like that because it feels more inviting. Working here while in college was probably the best thing for me. This company gave me an experience I may never of had anywhere else. If I had worked in a bigger company I wouldn't of had the same experience getting to know and getting to meet everyone.

SHOE CARNIVAL | 06/2020 to 06/2022

Shoe Carnival was my second job I started around when the covid restrictions were being lifted. I heard about this job from a friend that had been working there for a while and then she gave me a recommendation there. It was a fresh start after working at TJ's and a new experience that I never thought I would work in retail.

At shoe carnival I was responsible for stocking and running aisles, run the cash regester, and take care of the sock wall with the purse wall. I went through a remodel with shoe carnival working late nights to help put the store back together and cleaning up as the workers remodling were working. During the remodel everyone was stressed and everyone was under a lot of pressure to get everything just right. We had two or three weeks that everything had to be back up and running but there were late materials which had to be installed after we re-opened the store. It made working there a little crazy but I was able to make it through with the help of my coworkers.

Working at Shoe Carnival taught me to speak to people and listen closely to what a person actually needs. My favorite memory working there was this man came in looking for a pair of shoe for his sister who is a nurse. First I had recommeneded him Crocs. Reason for recommending them is he told me she was a nurse and most nurses that came in were looking for Crocs. He said no I really want to get her a tennis shoe and croc made a tennis shoe and I showed him those and he was a tad bit interested. Yet, he ended up leaving to get his sisters opinion. He came back maybe two weeks later and told me I gave him a good recommendation and that his sister actually had those shoes already. He also mentioned next time that he came in he better see me wearing them to give him my honest and genuine opinion.

I was sad to leave Shoe Carnival at first but at the same time it was good for my health. I was working two jobs and wasn't getting enough sleep at night. I made my final decision on leaving when I almost fell asleep at the wheel. I liked working two jobs though it gave me more of a reason to get up and not waste my days away. This job gave me many hours and I was very thankful for that I was very Thankful for my General Manager Misty Tucker at the time as well. She tried her best to make things work for me and for that I will always be thankful for.

TJ's Ice Cream Palace | 01/2016 to 01/2018

Oh TJ's what a fun place to work. It was a summer job that took up my time and with that it will always have a special place in my heart since it is closed. This was my first job and I worked for a lady name Pat Anthony. I was a cashier, food cook, and even manager for a day (it went well except on costumer didn't like how young I was). Working this job helped me learn the joy of multitasking because I had to juggle work and marching band at the same time and in the kitchen you might have to be cooking more then one order. This job set me up more so then school in my opinion to juggle a school life, work life, and a social life. The people I worked with were great probably the one job that hhad the least amount of drama you could imagine. I have to say though I feel somedays I had only gotten the job because of my brother and that he had worked there in high school before me, but Pat proved me wrong the next year when she asked me to come back and we worked out more of a deal with my marching band schedule. We had moments where we butt heads over it because if you had done marching band you know that takes up a lot of your time.

Pat once told me one of her favorite qualities of me was my patience and how I can handle when a new person just starting would make mistake after mistake. So, when someone new was just hired I would work that shift and she would tell me I will be training them. I tried my hardest to teach them as well as I could which I did but they would make little mistakes and I would show them how to fix them or they willl forget something and ask me real quick what it was. I do not like discouraging someone for what they are doing I like them to try it and come back to me to make sure it is correct especially for food orders. We had a girl that started around the saem time I did and would get food orders wrong and could not understand what she was doing worng. We would tell her what she did wrong but continue to do as she wished because she thought this is how it should be. Let's just say she did not last that long at TJ's sadly due to this, so when I was put to train someone I would make sure it was correct and that they were doing it right for maybe a week and a half after I let them go on their way.
